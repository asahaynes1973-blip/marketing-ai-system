She Used to Wake Up Ready. What Happened to Her?

If you've been feeling less like yourself — foggy, flat, or just off — it might not be what you think. Take 60 seconds to find out.

She was the one who got things done. Up early, clear-headed, running the day instead of being run by it. She had energy for the things that mattered. She was present.

If you're reading this, you might remember that version of yourself — and quietly wonder where she went.

You're not imagining the difference. And it might have less to do with stress or "just getting older" than you think.

What's actually happening

The changes are subtle at first. You snap at someone you love and feel bad about it afterward. You sit down to do something and forget what it was. By mid-afternoon, it takes everything you have just to keep going.

You don't feel sick. You just don't feel like you.

"I kept telling myself I was just tired. But it was more than that — I felt like I was disappearing a little. Less sharp, less present. Like there was a dimmer switch on everything I used to care about."

This pattern — the fogginess, the flatness, the sense of running below your usual level — is something a lot of women in their 40s and 50s describe. And for most of them, it's deeply connected to something that happens at night: the quality of their rest.

Not just the hours. The kind of sleep that actually restores you.

After 45, the body's ability to reach deep, restorative sleep begins to shift. When that happens, no amount of "good habits" fully compensates — and the effects show up not just as tiredness, but as you. Your clarity. Your patience. Your sense of being present in your own life.

You haven't lost yourself. Something may have been quietly stealing your rest — and with it, your energy to be who you are.
