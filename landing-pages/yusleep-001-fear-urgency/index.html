Why this matters more than you've been told

Sleep isn't just about feeling rested. Quality sleep is when the body does its most important recovery work — processing the day, supporting mood, clearing mental clutter, and maintaining the kind of energy that lets you show up fully for the things that matter to you.

When that recovery is consistently incomplete — night after night, month after month — it takes a toll on energy, mood, and clarity in ways that are easy to attribute to "just getting older" or "being stressed."

What researchers have noticed: The body's ability to achieve deep, restorative sleep doesn't remain constant over time — especially for women after 45. When the natural systems that support sleep quality begin to change, the effects show up gradually. And the longer the pattern runs, the harder it may be for the body to reset on its own.

Most women in this situation are also the last people to prioritize themselves. They take care of everyone and everything else — and their own rest is always the thing that gets pushed to later.
